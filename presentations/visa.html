Slide 02 · Market opportunity

Why this vertical, why now

  • EU first-residence permits 2024: ~3.5M issued, with employment as the leading reason (~31.9%).
  • Indian nationals: ~192k permits in 2024, employment dominant (~43.9% of Indian permits).
  • Germany Opportunity Card: Indians are the leading recipient nationality since launch.
  • Schengen friction: India ~15% rejection, Pakistan ~44% — strong demand for paid document support.
  • Implication: a sticky, high-intent audience that is willing to pay for clarity and reduced rejection risk.

Slide 07 · Channel strategy

Google vs Meta for visa intent

Google Search (primary)

  • Highest-intent users, ready to pay.
  • Faster keyword-level signal on ROI.
  • Best for paid package conversion.
  • CPC rises fast in immigration intent terms.
  • Strong negatives + landing relevance required.

Meta (secondary)

  • Cheap reach for problem-aware audiences.
  • Lead-form CTAs lower friction.
  • Great for retargeting + nurture.
  • Lower intent, more low-quality leads.
  • Faster creative fatigue.

Operating model: Meta discovers, Google captures, both retarget with the same message.

Slide 11 · Compliance & durability

White-hat checklist

  • Only partner with licensed migration/legal entities; pre-approve copy.
  • Disclose advisory vs legal services explicitly; no guaranteed approvals.
  • Refund and rejection-handling policy on every paid package.
  • One brand domain per region; consistent ad → prelander → checkout message.
  • Server-side tracking + CAPI for clean optimization signals.
  • Warm new ad accounts gradually; rotate creatives weekly.
  • Privacy/T&C/disclosure pages on every funnel.
  • Daily quality-ranking and disapproval monitoring.
